About Me

Hi, I'm Zach — a biologist, computing enthusiast, and geneticist.

I'm currently a Senior Bioinformatician at the Penn Medicine Biobank (PMBB), where I lead genomic pipeline development and data operations across the Setia-Verma, Verma, and Drivas labs at the Perelman School of Medicine, University of Pennsylvania. I completed my PhD at the Museum of Natural Science, Louisiana State University, investigating the genomic underpinnings of green-blooded lizards using phylogenomics and transcriptomics, followed by a postdoc in computational psychopathology analyzing digital phenotypes of serious mental illness.

I'm interested in integrating genomic, clinical EHR, and diverse demographic datasets to study the causes and treatments of human disease.
